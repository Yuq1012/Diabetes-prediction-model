    About dataset This dataset is originally from the National Institute of
Diabetes and Digestive and Kidney Diseases. The objective of the dataset
is to diagnostically predict whether a patient has diabetes based on
certain diagnostic measurements included in the dataset. Several
constraints were placed on the selection of these instances from a
larger database. In particular, all patients here are females at least
21 years old of Pima Indian heritage. In this project, we will find a
suitable machine learning model to predict the diabetes. In this dataset
there have several medical predictor variables and one target dependent
variable(outcome).

    \subsection{Conclusion}\label{conclusion}

In conclusion, the performance of four different machine learning models
were evaluated, including Logistic Regression, Support Vector Machine
(SVC), Decision Tree, and Random Forest, for the task of predicting
diabetes based on diagnostic measurements.

The Support Vector Machine (SVC) model demonstrated the highest accuracy
at 77.27\%, with an 83\% precision for non-diabetic cases and a 69\%
precision for diabetic cases. The Decision Tree model achieved an
accuracy of 70.13\%, offering balanced precision and recall values. The
Random Forest model achieved an accuracy of 72.73\%, with balanced
precision and recall.

The Logistic Regression model achieved an accuracy of 75.32\%, with a
65\% precision for both diabetic and non-diabetic cases. The model
performed consistently in terms of precision and recall for both
classes.

Based on the comparison of the four model results, the Support Vector
Machine (SVM) model seems as the most favorable choice for predicting
diabetes within the context of the analyzed data. The SVM model achieved
the highest accuracy among the evaluated models at 77.27\% and
demonstrated a balanced precision and recall for both diabetic and
non-diabetic cases. This suggests that SVM may offer the best trade-off
between correctly identifying diabetic cases and minimizing false
predictions.
